Firefox is Free

Learn about your web browser

Today, the most popular and well-respected Web browsers are free. Take Firefox, for example — it's easy to download and free for anyone to use. It just takes a few minutes to install: www.mozilla.org/firefox

What's a browser?

A browser is a piece of software that displays Web pages. Most popular browsers come installed at no additional charge on new computers, smartphones and tablets. You can also download them for free online, which takes only a few minutes.

Why do browsers matter?

The Web browser is the lens through which we look at the virtual world. A browser is software that turns a website's code into the visual images, links, and interactive functionality we see when we visit a website. The browser you choose is responsible for providing you with the tools to manage your online life, and protecting your privacy and security. The way you experience the Internet can be enhanced by a good browser, or limited by a bad one.

What is Mozilla?

Mozilla is a global non-profit, and the maker of Firefox. At Mozilla we build products, educate users, and advocate to keep the Internet alive and accessible, so that people worldwide can be informed contributors and creators of the Web. All of the software we build is always free and open source, meaning it's non-proprietary and its code is available to everyone. We will never charge you to use Firefox. Read more about our mission here: www.mozilla.org/mission
