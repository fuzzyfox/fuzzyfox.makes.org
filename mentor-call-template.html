Webmaker Mentor Team Call report-out

(4 December 2013)

Etherpad: teach.etherpad.mozilla.org/mentorcall3

TL;DR

This week our main focus was on next week's Hour of Code. We also welcomed the multi-talented Atul Varma to the team and talked about workflows in various forms.

Obstacles overcome in 2013

As a warm-up while people joined the call, we listed our 'favourite obstacle overcome in 2013'. These included:

  • Expanding from 19 to 40 members of Hive Toronto (Kathryn)
  • Learning Mozilla jargon within a month (Elsa)
  • Federating the Mozfest program so that Space Wranglers had complete programmatic control of the sessions, space design and facilitators for their tracks. (Michelle)

Everyone laughed with Elsa thinking that she'd now 'got' Mozilla jargon after only a month. Many of us are still learning several years in...

Introduction

Chris kicked things off by explaining that it's there's a meeting of the Mozilla Board today after which we'll have more clarity about funding and structure going forward for 2014. As a result, we'll have a 1.5 hour meeting next week to really dig into our individual and team goals.

Hour of Code

Next week it's the official kick-off of the Hour of Code which Mozilla is involved with. Lainie shared a draft blog post about our involvement and shared some things we can be doing:

  • Tweet links to Webmaker projects with the hashtag #hourofcode
  • File bugs from the tutorials on BugZilla
  • Get involved in a Connected Learning webinar this Thursday preparing for Hour of Code (features Doug)

Hive Toronto and Hive Chicago shared what they're up to with the Hour of Code - namely working with their networks to use Webmaker and other tools to teach young people some JavaScript.

It's also worth noting that there's a new 'tutorial' feature in Thimble that adds an additional pane to the left of the window with some explanatory text. Laura has written a great guide on how to get started with this!

Open position: Documentation & Design Coordinator

Will shared an open position for a Documentation & Design Coordinator for the Hive Gigabit Community Fund. He encouraged us to suggest people who might be interested and to contact him with networks who might be able to advertise the position more widely.

help@webmaker.org

Laura raised a new problem we have now that a couple of people have left Mozilla - namely that no-one is currently responsible for responding to 'help' emails from the community.

After some conversation, Amira volunteered to follow this up by digging out a workflow used previously and get together a plan that she will share back to the team at a later date.

Productivity call

After some great chat on the etherpad during last week's call there was an appetite for a separate productivity-themed call. Doug got in touch with those interested and a date/time has been set:

  • Date: Tuesday, 10th December 2013
  • Time: 8am PT / 11am ET / 4pm GMT / 5pm CET
  • Location: Vidyo (Doug's room)

Everyone welcome!

The conversation that followed got us onto documenting the 'tacit' knowledge within Mozilla. We flagged using the Foundation wiki and the Mozilla wiki more effectively as something to explore.
